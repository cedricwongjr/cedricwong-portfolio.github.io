Community Service


I was part of a community service project in school, helping out at Orange Valley nursing home. My classmates and I planned, prepared, and held a carnival for the elderly there. After much discussion and deliberation, we came up with a few games we thought the elderly would enjoy. We then split ourselves into several groups, each in charge of preparing a different game booth. The booth my group was in charge of was throwing a ball at a target. We designed the setup of the booth, made target boards out of cardboard, and even coordinated with the PE department to borrow some plastic balls.

All our hard work came to fruition on the day of the carnival. Interacting with the elderly on this scale was a new experience for me, but I quickly learned how to best be accommodating towards them. Some elderly were wheelchair-bound, which meant we had to escort them around, while we explained the rules patiently to others with dementia as many times as they needed. Overall, we did our best to ensure every elderly would be able to enjoy the carnival to their hearts' content. We were thus heartened to see that they really enjoyed the activities we set up for them, as many of them kept coming back to play their favourite game booths again and again. From this, I was able to experience firsthand how community service can really make a difference in others' lives — even if only for a day, seeing how happy the elderly were at the carnival made the weeks of hard work worth it.

Science Talent Management Programme

I was a member of my school's Science Talent Management Programme. I was selected to join in Secondary 2 based on my excellent grades in science as well as a short essay I wrote on a science-related topic of interest. Over the course of the programme, I participated in many science workshops on varied subjects. Two of the most memorable workshops for me were the NanoScience workshop where I carved my initials into a strand of hair, and a Forensic Science workshop where I learnt more about how the police use science to investigate a crime scene. It was a very fulfilling experience as I was able to further my scientific interests and gain a deeper understanding of science beyond the classroom.

As part of the programme, I had the opportunity to participate in the Science Buskers Competition. Participants had to create a skit or a presentation that showcased a scientific concept in an interesting way, and our projects were evaluated by a panel of judges as well as the general public. I paired up with a classmate who was also in the programme, and we came up with a 'magic trick' involving an hourglass and a beaker which demonstrated the concepts of centre of gravity and friction.
